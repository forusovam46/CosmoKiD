Our Solar System and the Milky Way

Our Solar System is a fascinating place. It includes the Sun, which is a star, and everything that orbits around it like planets, moons, asteroids, and comets. The Sun's gravity holds the Solar System together and keeps everything moving in its path.

Planets

There are eight main planets in our Solar System: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. Each of these planets is unique in its size, composition, and distance from the Sun. For example, Earth is the only planet we know of that has life, while Jupiter is the largest planet with a storm that has been raging for centuries.

Other Objects

Apart from the planets, our Solar System has many other interesting objects. These include dwarf planets like Pluto, the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter filled with rocky bodies, and the Kuiper Belt and Oort Cloud at the edge of the Solar System, which contain icy bodies and comets.

Eclipses

An eclipse happens when one object in space moves into the shadow of another object. On Earth, we experience solar eclipses when the Moon passes between the Earth and the Sun, blocking out the Sun’s light, and lunar eclipses when the Earth passes between the Sun and the Moon, casting a shadow on the Moon.

Solar System in Motion

Our Solar System isn't just static; it’s always moving. The planets orbit the Sun, and moons orbit the planets. But that's not all—the entire Solar System is also moving through space, orbiting the center of our galaxy, the Milky Way. This means that while the planets are moving around the Sun, the whole Solar System is making its way through the galaxy at an incredible speed.

Sizes and Distances

The sizes and distances in the Solar System can be hard to imagine. The images are not to scale. The planets are much smaller compared to the vast distances between them. For example, if the Sun were the size of a basketball, Earth would be about the size of a pea and 26 meters away!

Our Position in the Milky Way

The Milky Way is a vast galaxy that contains our Solar System, along with billions of other stars, planets, and celestial objects. Our Solar System is located in one of the outer spiral arms of the Milky Way, called the Orion Arm.
